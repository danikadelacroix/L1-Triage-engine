1.2 How to Raise an IT Ticket
Policy ID: IT-002  |  Category: IT Support  |  Last Revised: March 2025
Employees must raise tickets through the official support portal. Walk-in requests are accepted only at the IT Help Desk located at Floor 2, Block B of the Bengaluru HQ. Remote employees must use the portal or email.

Steps to Raise a Ticket
1.	Navigate to support.novatech.in and log in using your NovaTech SSO credentials.
2.	Click 'New Ticket' and select the relevant category (Hardware / Software / Network / Access / Other).
3.	Fill in the Title, Description, and Severity Level. Attach screenshots if applicable.
4.	Click Submit. You will receive a ticket ID via email (format: NT-XXXXXX) within 5 minutes.
5.	Track your ticket status under 'My Tickets'. You will be notified at each status change.

Important: Do not email individual IT team members directly. All tickets must go through the portal to ensure proper SLA tracking.

1.3 Password Reset Procedure
Policy ID: IT-003  |  Category: Access Management  |  Last Revised: March 2025
Employees can reset their NovaTech domain password through the self-service portal without raising a ticket. This includes passwords for Windows login, Outlook, and all SSO-connected applications.

Self-Service Password Reset (SSPR)
6.	Visit sso.novatech.in/reset from any device.
7.	Enter your registered employee email (format: firstname.lastname@novatech.in).
8.	Complete identity verification via OTP sent to your registered mobile number.
9.	Set a new password meeting the complexity requirements below.

Password Policy Requirements
•	Minimum 12 characters
•	Must include: 1 uppercase, 1 lowercase, 1 number, 1 special character (!@#$%^&*)
•	Cannot reuse the last 10 passwords
•	Passwords expire every 90 days. A reminder is sent 14 days before expiry.
•	Account locks after 5 consecutive failed attempts. Unlocks automatically after 30 minutes or via helpdesk.

If SSPR fails (e.g., mobile number not registered), employees must visit the IT Help Desk with their employee ID card for manual verification and reset.

VPN Installation

12.	Log in with your NovaTech SSO credentials + MFA token (Google Authenticator or hardware token).

VPN Troubleshooting — Common Issues
Issue	Likely Cause	Resolution
Cannot connect to VPN	Wrong server address or MFA misconfigured	Verify server is vpn.novatech.in; re-sync MFA token
VPN connects but internal sites unreachable	Split tunneling misconfiguration	Disconnect, reconnect, select correct profile
MFA OTP invalid	Device clock out of sync	Sync device time; contact helpdesk if persists
VPN account locked	Too many failed login attempts	Raise SEV-3 ticket or call helpdesk

3.1 Leave Policy
Policy ID: HR-001  |  Category: Leave Management  |  Last Revised: March 2025
NovaTech's leave policy is designed to ensure employees can maintain a healthy work-life balance. All leaves must be applied for in Workday (workday.novatech.in) at least 24 hours in advance, except for emergencies.

Leave Entitlements (Annual, Per Calendar Year)
Leave Type	Entitlement	Notes

Casual Leave (CL)	6 days	Not combinable with PL or Public Holidays. Cannot be carried forward.
Maternity Leave (ML)	26 weeks	As per Maternity Benefit Act, 1961. Applicable to female employees with >80 days of service.

Bereavement Leave (BL)	5 days	For immediate family (spouse, parents, children, siblings). Extendable by 3 days with manager approval.

Leave Application Process
19.	Log in to Workday at workday.novatech.in.

Leave Without Pay (LWP)	If all applicable leave balances are exhausted, additional absences are treated as LWP and salary is deducted proportionally. LWP requires HR approval for periods >3 consecutive days.

Section 4: Grievance Handling & Escalation
4.1 Grievance Policy Overview
Policy ID: HR-010  |  Category: Grievance Management  |  Last Revised: March 2025
NovaTech is committed to providing a respectful, fair, and inclusive workplace. Employees who experience issues — whether related to work conditions, interpersonal conflicts, policy violations, or harassment — have the right to raise a grievance without fear of retaliation.

All grievances are treated as confidential. Retaliation against any employee for raising a grievance in good faith is a serious violation of NovaTech's Code of Conduct and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

Types of Grievances Covered
•	Workplace harassment (verbal, physical, or psychological)
•	Sexual harassment (handled separately under POSH Policy — see Section 4.4)
•	Discrimination based on gender, religion, caste, age, disability, or sexual orientation
•	Unfair treatment, favoritism, or biased performance evaluation
•	Policy violations by managers or peers
•	Retaliation for whistleblowing or internal complaints
•	Work environment concerns (unsafe conditions, unreasonable workload)
•	Payroll or compensation disputes not resolved at HR Operations level

4.2 Grievance Escalation Path
Policy ID: HR-011  |  Category: Grievance Management  |  Last Revised: March 2025
Employees are encouraged to resolve issues at the lowest possible level. The following escalation path must be followed unless the grievance involves the person at that level (in which case, skip to the next level).

Level	Contact / Forum	Timeline
Level 1 — Informal	Direct conversation with immediate manager	Within 5 working days of incident
Level 2 — HR Business Partner	HRBP assigned to your BU (hrbp@novatech.in)	HRBP acknowledges within 2 days, resolves within 15 days
Level 3 — Grievance Committee	Formal Grievance Committee (3-member panel)	Hearing within 10 days, resolution within 30 days
Level 4 — HR Director	HR Director (hrdirector@novatech.in)	Decision within 15 days, binding on all parties
Level 5 — External (Labor Court)	State Labour Commissioner or Labour Court	Per applicable labor law timelines

4.3 How to Raise a Formal Grievance
Policy ID: HR-012  |  Category: Grievance Management  |  Last Revised: March 2025
A formal grievance can be raised when informal resolution has failed or when the nature of the complaint (e.g., harassment, discrimination) warrants skipping to formal channels.

Submission Methods
•	Online Portal: grievance.novatech.in — accessible 24x7, supports anonymous submissions
•	Email: grievance@novatech.in — encrypted inbox, accessible only to HR Director and POSH Committee Head
•	Physical Submission: Sealed envelope addressed to 'HR Grievance Cell, People Operations' deposited in secure drop-boxes on each floor

Information to Include in a Grievance
32.	Your name and employee ID (or 'Anonymous' for anonymous submissions — note: anonymous submissions limit investigative options)
33.	Name(s) of the person(s) involved
34.	Date, time, and location of the incident(s)
35.	Detailed description of what occurred
36.	Names of any witnesses
37.	Any prior steps taken to resolve informally
38.	Desired resolution or outcome

Anonymous Grievances	Anonymous grievances will be investigated to the extent possible, but NovaTech cannot guarantee remediation or communicate outcomes to anonymous submitters. Employees are encouraged to use named submissions for faster resolution.

Post-Submission Process
39.	Acknowledgement sent within 1 business day (for named submissions).
40.	HRBP assigned and preliminary review completed within 3 business days.
41.	If escalated to Grievance Committee: Committee constituted within 7 days. Both parties interviewed. Written submissions allowed.
42.	Final resolution communicated in writing. Remediation actions (if any) implemented within 15 days of decision.
43.	Appeal against Grievance Committee decision must be filed with the HR Director within 10 days of receiving the decision.

4.4 POSH Policy (Prevention of Sexual Harassment)
Policy ID: HR-013  |  Category: POSH / Sexual Harassment  |  Last Revised: March 2025
NovaTech fully complies with the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013. A separate Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) exists for POSH complaints and operates independently of the general grievance process.

ICC Contact	posh-icc@novatech.in  |  ICC Presiding Officer: Priya Nair (People Operations VP)  |  External Member: Adv. Sunita Rao

•	POSH complaints must be filed within 3 months of the incident (extendable to 6 months with ICC approval).
•	ICC has 90 days to complete inquiry from date of complaint receipt.
•	Both complainant and respondent have the right to present their case and be heard.
•	All ICC proceedings are strictly confidential. Violation of confidentiality is a punishable offense.
•	Conciliation may be attempted as first step (at complainant's request only).
•	Interim relief (e.g., transfer, leave) can be requested while investigation is ongoing.

4.5 Whistleblower Protection
Policy ID: HR-014  |  Category: Whistleblower  |  Last Revised: March 2025
Employees who report genuine concerns about financial misconduct, fraud, data privacy violations, or ethical breaches are protected under NovaTech's Whistleblower Policy. Reports can be made to ethics@novatech.in or anonymously via the Ethics Hotline: 1800-XXX-XXXX (toll-free, 24x7).

•	Whistleblowers are protected from retaliation, demotion, unfair appraisal, or termination for good-faith reporting.
•	False or malicious reporting with intent to harm others is itself a violation of the Code of Conduct.
•	The Ethics Committee (chaired by the CFO and Legal Counsel) reviews all whistleblower complaints within 30 days.

Section 6: Data Security & Acceptable Use Policy
6.1 Data Classification
Policy ID: IT-020  |  Category: Data Security  |  Last Revised: March 2025
All data handled by NovaTech employees must be classified according to the following framework. Handling requirements differ by classification level.

Classification	Definition	Handling Requirements
Public	Data intended for public release (marketing, press releases)	No restrictions
Internal	Operational data for employee use only (policies, internal comms)	Do not share externally without approval
Confidential	Business-sensitive data (financials, contracts, roadmaps)	Encrypted storage, NDA required for third-party sharing
Restricted	Highly sensitive (customer PII, source code, credentials)	Encrypted at rest and in transit, access logged, MFA required

6.2 Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)
Policy ID: IT-021  |  Category: Acceptable Use  |  Last Revised: March 2025
NovaTech's IT resources (devices, network, software, email, internet access) are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use is acceptable but must not interfere with work or violate this policy.

Prohibited Activities (Zero Tolerance)
•	Accessing, downloading, or distributing illegal content of any kind
•	Using company devices or email for personal business activities
•	Installing keyloggers, spyware, or any surveillance software
•	Attempting to bypass security controls, firewalls, or DLP systems
•	Sharing login credentials with anyone, including colleagues
•	Using company AI tools (Copilot, ChatGPT Enterprise) to process Restricted-class data without explicit InfoSec approval
•	Connecting personal USB devices to company laptops without IT approval

Monitoring & Privacy Notice
Employees are informed that NovaTech monitors network traffic, email metadata, and device activity logs for security purposes using automated tools (SIEM: Microsoft Sentinel; EDR: CrowdStrike). Individual content is not reviewed without a formal security incident or HR investigation approval. All monitoring is compliant with applicable Indian IT and labor laws.

6.3 Incident Reporting
Policy ID: IT-022  |  Category: Security Incidents  |  Last Revised: March 2025
Employees must report any suspected security incident immediately. Failure to report a known incident is a violation of the AUP.

What to Report
•	Phishing emails — forward to phishing@novatech.in and do not click any links
•	Malware or ransomware symptoms (slow device, unexpected popups, encrypted files)
•	Lost or stolen devices
•	Unauthorized access to your account (unexpected login alerts)
•	Accidental sharing of Confidential or Restricted data with wrong recipients

How to Report
50.	Call the Security Hotline immediately: +91-80-4567-8902 (24x7 for SEV-1/2)
51.	Raise a ticket at support.novatech.in with category 'Security Incident'.
52.	Do NOT attempt to investigate or remediate on your own. Isolate the affected device by disabling Wi-Fi.
53.	Preserve evidence — do not delete suspicious emails or files until IT Security advises.

Safe Harbour	Employees who report security incidents promptly and in good faith will not face disciplinary action for accidental incidents (e.g., clicking a phishing link). Prompt reporting is always rewarded.
